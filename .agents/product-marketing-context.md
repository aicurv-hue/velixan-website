## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Founders and small business owners have real ideas and real operational problems but no trusted technical team to turn to — and they fear being burned by agencies that overpromise, under-deliver, or speak in jargon.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Freelancers on Upwork are unpredictable and hard to manage without technical knowledge.
- Large agencies are too expensive, too slow, and treat small clients as low priority.
- Off-the-shelf software doesn't fit specific business workflows.
**What it costs them:** Delayed launches, wasted budgets, half-built products, and manual work that eats up the team's time.
**Emotional tension:** "I don't know how to evaluate if the work is good." / "I've been burned before." / "I don't want to spend months and get nothing."

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Other software development agencies — fall short by using jargon, hiding juniors behind senior faces, and lacking clear communication for non-technical clients.
**Indirect:** Upwork freelancers or in-house hires — fall short due to unpredictability, lack of accountability, and inability to scale with the business.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- 7-day free trial with real output before any financial commitment.
- Plain-English communication — no jargon, ever.
- Senior engineers on every project, no bait-and-switch with juniors.
- Built for non-technical founders: they describe the problem, we handle everything else.
**How we do it differently:** We work like an in-house team, not an outsourcing vendor. Founders get visibility, accountability, and a team that treats their product like their own.
**Why customers choose us:** They don't have to know tech to work with us — and they can try before they buy.
